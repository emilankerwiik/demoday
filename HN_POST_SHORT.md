# Show HN: Demoday — clickable demos, no auth, no videos, no animations

---

I built Demoday because I got tired of the demo friction. Screen recordings bore people. Signup walls kill engagement. Fancy animations waste time. What people actually want is to click through your product in 30 seconds.

**The problem in three scenarios:**

1. **Giving your phone to a stranger.** You're demoing at a conference. You hand your phone to someone. They have to log in, remember your password, navigate the real app. That's three friction points. They bail.

2. **Outbound sales while on the call.** You're on a sales call. You want to show the customer what the product does. You share your screen, click around for 90 seconds. Now they're watching a video of you using it, not using it themselves. They're passively consuming instead of actively exploring.

3. **Website visitors who won't sign up first.** 95% of your visitors leave your landing page without trying the product because signup is friction. You want them to click through right there on the page and see what the product does. No auth. No waiting.

**What Demoday does:** Generate a clickable demo from your code and embed it on your landing page with one iframe. No video. No authentication. No animations. Just click the buttons, see what happens.

**The three use cases:**

- Hand your phone to a prospect, investor, or conference attendee. They click through your product in 30 seconds. No login required.
- Paste a link during an outbound sales call. They click through while you're talking, not you clicking for them.
- Embed it on your landing page. Visitors understand what you do in under a minute. No signup wall.

**How it works:** One npm install. Ask your coding agent to run it. Done. Generates one HTML file, embeds it with an iframe. The demo reads your actual codebase, so it stays in sync.
